Rosemarie's Baby

This book by Ira Levin encapsulates the meaning of suspense in every word of the book.
Masterfully woven, the tale follows a husband and wife couple, focused on Rosemarie herself,
as they move into their first house together. However, they are warned against moving to this home
on account of witchery and other wicked things making it their domain,
and as she herself finds, more and more strange, disturbing, and painful events
keep happening around her, just as she becomes pregnant. Struggling in the uncertainty of
both motherhood and the trust in those around her, the audience is pressed with suspense and horror
as we witness the story of Rosemarie's baby.
A beautiful mix of cultist, witchcraft, suspense, and popular panic
built on the worries and concerns of his time, Levin crafts a masterpiece of horror
that redefines the meaning of suspense, and instills a different, subtle, sinister terror.
