The Rats in the Walls

This story, by the now-famous H.P. Lovecraft, centers around the return to a
ancestral home, if you would. The village surrounding this home regards it,
and the family who was housed in it, with a strange hatred.
Rather than drive off the protagonist, it urges him forward
into a tenebrous, dark past, riddled with spiritual and cosmic horror
that threatens to break him as he goes deeper and deeper into the history
of his own familial home.
This story, while well-regarded, does suffer from some issues of the time.
Nevertheless, the merit of the author later redeems this,
and the story itself is an interesting, inquiring look at horror from history,
and horror from the stars.
