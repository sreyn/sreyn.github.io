Bram Stoker

Bram Stoker was an irish author, known best for writing the ageless tale of Dracula in 1897.
In reality, during his life, he was better known as an assistant to Henry Irving and the manager of
the Lyceum Theatre, one of Irving's holdings. While he is best known for Dracula, he wrote a number of other
works, including The Snake's Pass, The Lady of the Shroud, and The Lair of the White Worm.
There is much more to be known about the author, including his friendships with other
very well known authors, including Oscar Wilde, and his inspiration for his novels, if one is so interested.
