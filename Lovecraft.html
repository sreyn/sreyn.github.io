H.P. Lovecraft

Howard Phillips Lovecraft, more well-known by the shorthand H.P. Lovecraft,
was an American writer of "weird fiction" and horror,
and eventually he would become known as the pioneer or Father of a new subculture within the
horror and fiction genre: cosmic horror. His type of horror didn't revolve around the horrors existing,
or coming from, Earth and her holds. Rather, his focus was on the stars, on the cosmos, and highlighted
an accute form of existentialist dread, focusing on a horror of uncertainty and loss at beings and threats
that we as humans couldn't possibly begin to understand. While meticulously detailed in his works,
the objects of horror were always changing, shifting, unable to pin down and yet ever-present.
While he died in 1937 as a little-known author, he is now reverred as a king of the cosmic horror.

"That is not dead which can eternal lie, And with strange aeons even death may die."
